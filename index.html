Message from Nicholas: February 1, 2020

Dear Friends and Supporters,

It’s been about two years since I’ve reached out to all of you. I'd like to first thank everyone for contributing to my treatment funds and helping me heal over these years. You are all such kind and giving people, and your generosity moves me deeply; I feel you’ve saved my life many times. I can’t express my gratitude in words. Second, I’ve asked my friends to write a letter on my behalf, to share my update and to request your help again.

I had been in a healing community for about a year, living alongside good people and receiving treatment there. I experienced again some improvements in my cognitive ability, energy level, and hope, and I felt positive for the first time in a long time. However, the center has since closed, and a few months later, I relapsed. Now I'm back to square one—I'm in need of finding a suitable treatment center.

I have decided to pursue treatment from Lymexico, led by Dr. Morales, the only ILADS certified doctor in Mexico, who is using ground-breaking techniques to treat people suffering from chronic Lyme and other bacterial infections. I’m confident and hopeful that they are reliable and capable of helping address my extremely complex case. He was the first doctor I spoke with who gave a clear and comprehensive diagnosis, explaining the reason for my individualized treatment protocol, based on my specific case.

To begin this treatment, funds are needed. The goal is $33,000, which should cover the initial intensive treatment and some subsequent costs. Health insurance doesn’t cover any of this kind of care, unfortunately. (See article here.) And it’s costly, as are most modern medical treatments. So with great humility, I again request your generosity, to help me recover from this debilitating disease.

Again, I thank you for your donations. You are not only supporting me, but are supporting a population of people suffering from Lyme who all deserve the opportunity to recover.

Chronology

In 2011 Nicholas was diagnosed with Lyme Disease and given oral antibiotics.

In 2012, he began to develop more serious symptoms including chronic fatigue.

In 2013, a spinal tap confirmed Lyme had spread to his brain - neurological Lyme Disease. He was given an intravenous treatment of antibiotics which lasted four months during 2013 and 2014.

Since the winter of 2014 he has been taking herbal supplements, has been unable to work, and is on Medicaid which unfortunately does not cover Lyme treatments beyond what had already been done.

We appreciate any generosity that you can show in support of this young man's life.

At the end of Nicholas’s treatment, any remaining funds will be donated to institutions doing research on Lyme Disease and its treatment.
